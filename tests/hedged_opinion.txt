There is a reasonable case that AI development may proceed faster than
current regulation can adapt. Some labs publish capabilities reports;
others remain opaque, which limits external review.

According to the Stanford AI Index 2024, training compute roughly doubles
every six months, though the report's methodology section notes selection
bias in which labs disclose figures. A counterpoint: economic gravity
tends to slow disruptive technologies once liability and insurance markets
catch up, so the curve may plateau.

I think the tradeoff between speed and safety is real, but I am uncertain
about the timing. The mechanism by which regulation lags innovation is
plausible but not proven, and alternative explanations exist.
