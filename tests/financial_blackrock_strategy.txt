BlackRock Investment Institute — Global Investment Outlook, November 2024

OVERVIEW
We upgrade developed market (DM) equities to overweight. The strategic case rests on a resilient earnings backdrop, continued AI-driven productivity gains, and a moderated rate cycle. We believe the risk-reward favors equities over investment-grade bonds at current valuations.

EQUITY ALLOCATION
Developed market equities: upgraded to overweight from neutral.
  U.S. equities: overweight. S&P 500 earnings per share projected at $245-$255 in 2025 (consensus: $243). AI capital expenditure cycle supports technology and industrials.
  Japan equities: overweight on earnings upgrade cycle and yen normalization.
  European equities: neutral. Structural headwinds from energy costs and weak German industrial output remain.
  UK equities: neutral. Valuation remains cheap but growth outlook is constrained.
Emerging market equities: neutral. Dollar strength and China uncertainty limit near-term upside.

FIXED INCOME ALLOCATION
High yield credit: downgraded to neutral from overweight. Spreads at 310 bps offer limited buffer relative to default risk in a slower-growth scenario.
Investment grade credit: neutral. All-in yields attractive but duration risk remains elevated.
DM government bonds: underweight. We expect 10-year Treasury yields to range between 4.25% and 4.75% in 2025; limited capital appreciation at current levels.
Inflation-linked bonds (TIPS and linkers): overweight. Residual inflation risk in services and shelter justifies real-rate exposure.

TACTICAL VIEWS
We are positioning for a soft landing, not a hard landing. Our tactical tilt is toward pro-cyclical sectors: technology, industrials, and select financials. We are reducing duration exposure in sovereign bonds and increasing allocation to real assets.

MARKET BACKDROP
S&P 500 reached a record high of 5,894 on November 11, 2024.
10-year Treasury yield: 4.56%.
Brent crude: $72.10 per barrel.
Gold: $2,578 per ounce.

RISK FACTORS
Tactical views could be invalidated by: a significant re-acceleration of U.S. inflation, geopolitical disruption to energy supply, or a sharper-than-expected slowdown in China.
