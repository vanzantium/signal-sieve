T. Rowe Price Global Markets Weekly Update — Week of November 11, 2024

UNITED STATES
Consumer Price Index (CPI): +2.6% year-over-year in October, up from +2.4% in September. Core CPI held at +3.3%. The reacceleration in headline inflation was driven by shelter and energy components.
Producer Price Index (PPI): +2.4% year-over-year in October, above the +2.2% consensus estimate.
Retail sales: +0.4% month-over-month, suggesting resilient consumer spending.
Initial jobless claims: 217,000 for the week ending November 9, in line with forecasts.
10-year Treasury yield: 4.45%, up 14 basis points on the week.

Federal Reserve Commentary: Chicago Fed President Austan Goolsbee said in a Bloomberg interview on November 13: "We still have a ways to go. I'm not seeing signs that we should pause the easing cycle, but the data have to cooperate."

EUROPE
Eurozone GDP growth: +0.4% quarter-over-quarter in Q3 2024, beating the +0.2% consensus.
German industrial production: -2.0% month-over-month in September, underscoring structural weakness.
ECB policy outlook: Markets are pricing three 25 bps cuts by mid-2025, though ECB officials remain data-dependent.
STOXX 600: -1.2% on the week. FTSE 100 (UK): +0.3%.

JAPAN
Bank of Japan left rates unchanged at 0.25% at its October meeting. Governor Ueda signaled no urgency to hike further.
Japan CPI: +2.3% year-over-year in September. Core CPI ex-food and energy: +2.1%.
Nikkei 225: -2.8% on the week, pressured by yen weakening.
USD/JPY: 156.4, near a three-month high for dollar strength.

CHINA
Caixin Manufacturing PMI: 50.3 in October (previous: 49.3), back into expansion territory.
China exports rose 12.7% year-over-year while imports fell 2.3%, widening the trade surplus to $95.3 billion.
PBOC left its one-year loan prime rate at 3.10%. Authorities pledged additional fiscal stimulus in Q4.
CSI 300 (mainland China equities): +1.8% on the week.

EMERGING MARKETS
MSCI Emerging Markets Index: -1.5% on the week, pressured by dollar strength post-U.S. election.
Brazil: Bovespa -3.1% on fiscal concerns following government spending announcements.
India: Sensex +0.9%, supported by domestic consumption data.

SOURCES AND CAVEATS
This update summarizes publicly reported data as of market close November 13, 2024. Primary sources include: U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (CPI, PPI, jobless claims), U.S. Census Bureau (retail sales), Eurostat (European GDP), Japan Statistics Bureau (Japan CPI), China National Bureau of Statistics (PMI, trade data). Index and yield data via Bloomberg. All figures subject to revision.
