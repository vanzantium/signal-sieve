In my view, the Federal Reserve's rate policy is fundamentally broken. I believe the central bank is making a serious mistake by keeping rates elevated when inflation has clearly subsided.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, inflation fell to 2.4% in October 2024. Despite this data point, the Fed kept rates at 5.25 to 5.50 percent. Higher rates have contributed to a 23% drop in housing starts over the past twelve months. Some 5.2 million households are now in mortgage stress according to industry estimates.

The real issue is that policymakers appear to be fighting the last war. Tight money policy likely transfers wealth from workers to financial institutions. Momentum in the housing sector appears to be reversing. Sentiment among small business owners seems to have weakened considerably.

The Fed should cut rates immediately. They are causing unnecessary economic pain. What nobody in Washington will admit is that this policy is benefiting banks at the expense of ordinary households. In my opinion, this will be studied as a policy failure for decades to come.
